Here are the vote-rich areas for the 2022 Philippine polls

February 13, 2022

Election season is on in the Philippines, where voters are scheduled to troop to precincts by May 9 to elect a president, vice-president and other local and national leaders.
Campaigning is already underway, especially for 10 candidates looking to replace President Rodrigo Duterte who will step down from office noon of June 30.
But with limited time and over 2,000 inhabited islands, candidates are geared in luring voters in much coveted areas where they are heavily concentrated. This also makes for a good strategy as historically, it is not unusual for voters situated in similar geographical locations to favor overwhelmingly a candidate for various reasons, including that they hail from that area.
By the numbers however, a look at the Commission on Elections data released recently easily showed Metro Manila as home to a big chunk of voters.

Also called the National Capital Region (NCR), Metro Manila is home to over one in 10 registered voters for the 2022 polls, data showed. Broken down, the capital region is also home to the two cities where there are over 1 million voters: Quezon City and Manila, the capital.
Outside NCR, Duterte’s hometown, Davao City in Mindanao, is also home to nearly a million voters. Duterte’s daughter, Sara Duterte-Carpio, is running for vice-president.
Her running mate Ferdinand “Bongbong” Marcos Jr., son of the late dictator and current front-runner, hails from Ilocos province, north of the Philippines. That province has over 3.5 million registered voters.
In Cebu, the largest city down south, over 713,000 voters are registered. In neighboring Iloilo, 330,470 were registered. Both cities, and the rest of the Visayas, are touted as bailiwick of Vice President Leni Robredo, the opposition leader challenging Marcos.
Marcos lost to Robredo in the 2016 polls by over 200,000 votes.
Back in Metro Manila, Caloocan City follow QC and Manila with over 700,000 registered voters, data showed. Makati, the central financial district, has over 458,000. Prinz Magtulis
